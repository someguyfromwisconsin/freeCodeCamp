Norm Macdonald

Here is a brief timeline of his life:

  • 1959 - Norman Gene Macdonald is born on October 17 in Quebec City, Quebec, Canada.
  • 1981 - Graduates from Algonquin College in Ottawa, Canada, with a degree in communications.
  • 1987 - Begins his comedy career in stand-up, eventually making appearances on "The Late Show with David Letterman" and "The Tonight Show with Johnny Carson."
  • 1993 - Joins the cast of "Saturday Night Live" and becomes the Weekend Update anchor, a role he holds until 1998.
  • 1998 - Stars in the sitcom "The Norm Show," which runs for three seasons.
  • 2006 - Releases his comedy album "Ridiculous," showcasing his unique and deadpan comedic style. 2011 - Publishes his memoir, "Based on a True Story: Not a Memoir."
  • 2013 - Launches the podcast "Norm Macdonald Live," featuring interviews with fellow comedians and celebrities.
  • 2018 - Netflix releases "Norm Macdonald Has a Show," a talk show hosted by Macdonald.
  • 2021 - Norm Macdonald passes away on September 14, after a private battle with cancer.
